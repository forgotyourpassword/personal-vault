# Harbor View Interview Rubric - Curiosity Under Stress

Purpose: Screen for high-skill candidates who stay constructive under ambiguity instead of becoming suspicious, reactive, or controlling.

Use this for partner, manager, and senior individual contributor interviews.

## Core competencies to score (1-5)

1) Ownership under ambiguity
- 1: Blames context/people, waits or escalates emotionally
- 3: Mix of ownership and blame
- 5: Quickly identifies what they can control and acts

2) Attribution style (intent)
- 1: Defaults to bad intent when context is missing
- 3: Sometimes charitable, sometimes cynical
- 5: Starts with neutral/charitable interpretation, then verifies

3) Curiosity before judgment
- 1: Reacts first, questions later (or never)
- 3: Asks some questions after conflict starts
- 5: Uses clarifying questions early to prevent conflict

4) Frustration-to-action conversion
- 1: Stews, vents, or withdraws
- 3: Eventually raises concerns but vaguely
- 5: Converts frustration into specific asks and next steps within 24 hours

5) Conflict repair behavior
- 1: Punishes, routes around people, or goes passive-aggressive
- 3: Partial repair
- 5: Addresses directly, resets trust, and restores working cadence

## Structured interview questions

Ask these exactly, then probe for one concrete example each.

1. "Tell me about a time you were left out of context on an important project. What did you assume first, and what did you do next?"
2. "Describe a leadership decision you disagreed with. How did you challenge it, and what did you do after the decision was made?"
3. "Tell me about a time you were frustrated with a coworker. What did you do to test your interpretation before reacting?"
4. "When you feel out of control at work, what is your personal 24-hour playbook?"
5. "Tell me about a time you were wrong about someone’s intent. What changed your view?"

## Scenario test (Marcus vs Dana pattern)

Prompt:
"You receive a Friday email that expands project scope. No one gave you context. Walk me through exactly what you do in the next 48 hours."

Look for:
- Clarifying outreach before accusation
- Calm escalation to the right person
- Specific request for alignment
- No revenge behavior, no withdrawal, no story-spinning

## Reference check question (must ask)

"Under stress and ambiguity, does this person become more collaborative or more suspicious/controlling? Can you give one specific example?"

## Decision rule

- Strong Hire: No competency below 3, average >= 4.0, and no major red-flag behaviors
- Hold: Any score of 2 in Ownership, Attribution, or Curiosity
- No Hire: Two or more competencies <= 2, or clear evidence of repeated bad-intent assumptions/unilateral control behavior under stress

## Red flags (automatic concern)

- "I knew what they were doing" with no verification behavior
- Repeated stories where others are always the problem
- Describes control tactics as leadership
- Cannot name a time they were wrong about intent
- Uses conflict language without repair actions

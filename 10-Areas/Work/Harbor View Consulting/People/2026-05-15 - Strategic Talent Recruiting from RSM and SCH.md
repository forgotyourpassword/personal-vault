# Strategic Talent Recruiting from RSM and SC&H

Captured: 2026-05-15
Type: Strategy thought
Related reading: [[Steve Jobs]] / Walter Isaacson, Chapter 14

## Source thought

While reading the Steve Jobs biography, Michael noted that Apple poached John Sculley, a top executive, from Pepsi. The analogy: Harbor View Consulting should consider intentionally recruiting top talent from strong regional competitors, especially **RSM** and **SC&H**.

Specific trigger:
- New hire Brad Hunt started 2026-05-04 and came from **RSM**.
- That suggests RSM may be a productive talent source for Harbor View's model, standards, and client base.
- SC&H is another likely source of people with relevant regional-market experience.

## Why this is useful

This is not about random hiring. The useful idea is to treat competitor talent acquisition as a deliberate growth lever. Harbor View can look for people who already understand the client profile, service expectations, and regional accounting/advisory market, then offer a better culture, more autonomy, stronger upside, or a more entrepreneurial seat.

The Sculley lesson is not "hire famous people." It is: when the next stage of growth requires capabilities the company does not yet have at scale, go find people who have already operated at that level.

## Possible recruiting angles

- Build a target list of RSM and SC&H alumni/current leaders in accounting advisory, outsourced CFO, transaction advisory, Workiva, nonprofit accounting, and quality of earnings.
- Use Brad Hunt's experience as a pattern detector: what made Harbor View attractive enough to leave RSM?
- Identify the seats where outside talent creates the most leverage: Manager, Senior Manager, Principal, Partner, niche practice leads.
- Create a "why Harbor View" recruiting narrative for people who are strong but tired of large-firm bureaucracy.
- Track firms of origin for successful hires so the recruiting thesis becomes evidence-based, not vibes.

## Guardrails

- Keep this ethical and reputation-safe: recruit people, do not solicit confidential information or client lists.
- Do not over-index on pedigree. RSM/SC&H background is a signal, not proof of fit.
- Screen hard for Harbor View values, autonomy with accountability, and ability to build inside a smaller firm.
- Avoid creating a culture of "big-firm refugee entitlement." The point is high-agency builders, not logo collectors.

## Next action idea

Create a small competitor-talent map:

1. Target firms: RSM, SC&H, YHB, regional boutiques.
2. Target seats: Manager through Partner-level advisory talent.
3. Target practices: accounting advisory, outsourced CFO, transaction advisory, Workiva, nonprofit finance.
4. Recruiting message: "more impact, less bureaucracy, builder seat, high standards."
5. Evidence loop: compare each source against hire quality, ramp speed, retention, and client impact.

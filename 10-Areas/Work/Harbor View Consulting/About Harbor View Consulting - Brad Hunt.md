# About Harbor View Consulting - Brad Hunt

Source: PDF prepared March 2026 for Brad Hunt, CPA.

## What this document is
A Harbor View positioning and strategy document covering:
- mission
- values
- 2026 strategic objectives
- how the firm earns business
- service playbooks
- alliance partners

## Key Takeaways
### Mission and identity
- Harbor View's mission is to build the firm the team wants to work for and defend that standard in daily decisions.
- The shared vision emphasizes empowered people, strategic partnership with clients, and making clients and teammates better than before they met Harbor View.
- The culture explicitly values autonomy with accountability.

### Values: CREDIT
- **Collaboration**
- **Results**
- **Efficiency**
- **Diversity, Inclusion & Belonging**
- **Iteration**
- **Transparency**

These are not framed as poster values. The document ties each one to concrete behaviors like writing decisions down, avoiding unnecessary meetings, acting on feedback quickly, and making context visible.

### 2026 strategic objectives surfaced in the document
The PDF lays out multiple growth platforms, including:
- building a premier **Quality of Earnings** practice in the Mid-Atlantic
- building a high-performing **Workiva implementation** practice in partnership with **Labrador**
- becoming the go-to provider of accounting and finance services for **Maryland-based non-profits**

### Service model themes
The Outsourced CFO playbook stresses:
- executive-level strategic finance without full-time CFO cost
- hands-on regional partnership
- KPI visibility, dashboards, board reporting, cash flow discipline, fundraising support, M&A advisory, and internal controls

### Alliance partner ecosystem
The document lists a tiered partner model with notable partners including:
- Workiva
- Labrador
- YHB
- Prophix
- Crossbeam
- Evergreen Advisors
- Singer Damareck
- Tipalti
- Vena Solutions
- Trullion
- Fieldguide
- Ceretax

It also states that Harbor View's Workiva credibility is grounded in prior delivery experience, major project volume, strong partner relationships, and repeated recognition.

## Why this matters for EOS work
This document gives a clearer picture of Harbor View's actual shape:
- strong vision language
- explicit values and culture design
- multiple strategic bets that will need prioritization and traction
- a clear need for accountability, process, scorecards, and meeting discipline as the firm grows

In plain English: this is a very useful "Vision" artifact. It also hints at where EOS pressure will show up first, especially around focus, seat clarity, operating cadence, and execution across several growth platforms.

## Character Sheet Implications
For Michael's EOS campaign, this document strengthens the case for **Visionary** as the primary class.

Why:
- the document is full of firm-shaping language, direction-setting, and strategic positioning
- it centers on building the firm Harbor View wants to become, not just running today's operations
- it reflects big-picture thinking across partnerships, offerings, market position, and culture

Likely Visionary strengths suggested by this artifact:
- strong orientation toward mission and long-range direction
- natural instinct for strategic positioning and ecosystem thinking
- high tolerance for ambiguity when building something new

Likely watchouts:
- too many simultaneous strategic fronts can dilute traction
- vision can outrun operating cadence
- strong ideas still need seat clarity, scorecards, rocks, and issue resolution to land

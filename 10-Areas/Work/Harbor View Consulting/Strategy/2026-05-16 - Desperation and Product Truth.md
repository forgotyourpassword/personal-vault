# Desperation and Product Truth

Captured: 2026-05-16
Type: Strategy / leadership thought
Related reading: [[Steve Jobs]] / Walter Isaacson

## Source thought

While reading the Steve Jobs biography, Michael noted that when Macintosh sales dipped near the end of 1984, Jobs put Macintosh emulation software on the unsold Lisa inventory and sold those machines as the **Macintosh XL**.

Michael's reaction:

> This was basically a hoax and a moment where Jobs acted out of desperation.

## Why this matters

This is a useful counterweight to the heroic Jobs narrative. The same force that can create taste, urgency, and product conviction can also turn into desperation when reality does not cooperate.

The leadership lesson is not just "move fast" or "find a creative way through inventory." It is that pressure can make a leader rationalize a story that is technically defensible but substantively misleading.

The danger zone is when a company starts solving a sales or inventory problem by bending the truth of the product.

## Operating principle

Do not let desperation rename the product.

A cleaner standard:

> If we have to repackage, reposition, or discount something, the story must still be honest about what the client is actually buying.

## Harbor View translation

For a services business, the equivalent risk is not leftover Lisa inventory. It is selling a capability, team, timeline, or quality level that the firm cannot honestly deliver because pipeline pressure, utilization pressure, or ego gets too loud.

Useful questions:

- Are we describing the thing as it is, or as we wish it were?
- Would a client feel misled if they saw the internal version of the story?
- Are we solving the real operating problem, or masking it with positioning?
- Is this creative packaging, or are we laundering a miss into a new label?

## Guardrails

- Repositioning is fine. Misrepresentation is not.
- Desperation should trigger narrower promises, not bigger claims.
- Inventory, capacity, and pipeline problems should be named internally before they become client-facing fiction.
- A clever save is not a win if it erodes trust.

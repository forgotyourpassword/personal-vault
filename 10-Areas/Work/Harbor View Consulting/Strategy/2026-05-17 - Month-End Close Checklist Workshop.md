# Month-End Close Checklist Workshop

Captured: 2026-05-17
Type: Productized consulting offer
Status: Draft idea

## Core idea

Harbor View Consulting offers a **two-day on-site training and facilitation workshop** that helps a client build a draft **month-end close checklist** specific to their current accounting process.

This is not just a generic training session and not just delivery of a template. Harbor View brings examples, teaches what belongs in a strong month-end close process, facilitates mapping of the client's existing activities, and leaves the client with a working draft checklist by the end of day two.

## Client promise

> In two days, your accounting team will understand what belongs in a disciplined month-end close and will walk away with a draft close checklist built around your actual process.

## Problem this solves

Many clients have month-end close activities scattered across people, spreadsheets, emails, recurring calendar reminders, and institutional memory. The work gets done, but the process is often undocumented, inconsistent, person-dependent, or hard to manage.

The workshop creates structure without requiring a months-long transformation project.

## Offer structure

### Before the workshop

Harbor View sends a short pre-work packet asking the client to gather:

- Existing close checklists, if any
- Recurring close schedules or calendars
- Key reconciliations
- Reporting deadlines
- Recurring journal entries
- Review and approval steps
- Known pain points or bottlenecks
- Names and roles of people involved in close

### Day 1: Teach and map

Objectives:

- Teach what a strong month-end close checklist includes
- Show example checklists at different maturity levels
- Define the client's close scope: entities, departments, accounts, reports, systems, and deadlines
- Map the client's existing close activities
- Identify missing steps, duplicate work, unclear ownership, and review gaps

Working sessions:

- What belongs in month-end close
- Close calendar and deadline mapping
- Task inventory by functional area
- Ownership mapping: preparer, reviewer, approver
- Risk and dependency identification

### Day 2: Build and refine

Objectives:

- Convert the mapped activities into a draft checklist
- Organize tasks by timing, owner, dependency, and review level
- Add evidence requirements and completion criteria
- Identify fast improvements for next close cycle
- Align leadership on how the checklist will be maintained

Working sessions:

- Draft checklist build-out
- Review and approval layer
- Exception handling and escalation rules
- Maintenance cadence
- Final walkthrough with management

## End deliverable

By the end of the two days, the client receives a draft month-end close checklist that includes:

- Task name
- Description / purpose
- Owner
- Reviewer / approver
- Due date or day-of-close timing
- Frequency
- Required support or evidence
- Source system or report
- Dependencies
- Completion criteria
- Open issues / improvement opportunities

## Guarantee angle

Possible guarantee:

> If the client does not have a draft month-end close checklist by the end of the two-day workshop, Harbor View refunds the workshop fee.

This is a strong guarantee because the deliverable is concrete and largely within Harbor View's facilitation control. It also signals confidence without promising that the close itself will be fixed in two days.

## Important guardrails

The guarantee should be tied to delivery of the **draft checklist**, not broader outcomes such as:

- Reducing close time by a specific number of days
- Fixing all accounting issues
- Eliminating audit adjustments
- Completing reconciliations
- Implementing software
- Achieving a perfect close process

A cleaner version:

> We guarantee you will leave the two-day workshop with a draft month-end close checklist tailored to your current process, assuming your team participates and provides the requested pre-work materials. If not, we refund the workshop fee.

## Pricing hypothesis

Potential price range: **$5,000 to $15,000**.

Suggested positioning:

- **$5,000 to $7,500**: small client, simple entity structure, limited prep, mostly training plus facilitated draft checklist
- **$10,000**: standard offer, two facilitators or one senior facilitator, tailored checklist, pre-work review, and post-workshop cleanup
- **$15,000**: complex client, multiple entities/departments, more customization, leadership readout, and one follow-up refinement session

My take: the best initial packaged price is probably **$10,000**. It is high enough to be taken seriously, low enough to avoid procurement drag, and still gives Harbor View room to deliver real value without creating a monster scope.

## Why this could work for Harbor View

- It converts advisory value into a clear, purchasable product.
- It has a concrete deliverable clients understand.
- It creates a natural wedge for follow-on accounting, controller, CFO, systems, or process improvement work.
- It helps prospects who know they have a messy close but do not yet want a large engagement.
- It positions Harbor View as practical operators, not slide-deck consultants.

## Follow-on opportunities

After the workshop, Harbor View can offer:

- Close checklist implementation support
- Reconciliation cleanup
- Close calendar redesign
- Controller support
- Accounting policy documentation
- Month-end close dashboarding
- NetSuite / QuickBooks / Sage process improvements
- Audit readiness support
- Recurring monthly close advisory

## Sales language

Short version:

> We come on-site for two days and help your accounting team turn the month-end close from tribal knowledge into a working checklist. We bring examples, train your team on what a good close process includes, map what you already do, and leave you with a draft checklist tailored to your business.

More direct version:

> If your month-end close lives in people's heads, emails, and old spreadsheets, this workshop turns it into an operating system.

# SEPARATION AGREEMENT AND GENERAL RELEASE

This Separation Agreement and General Release (the "Agreement") is made by and between Harbor View Consulting LLC (the "Company") and Danielle Rommè ("Employee"). The Company and Employee are referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Employee was employed by the Company as a Partner; and

WHEREAS, Employee's employment with the Company will terminate effective April 16, 2026 (the "Separation Date"); and

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to resolve any and all claims arising out of or relating to Employee's employment and the separation of that employment, on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises, covenants, and releases contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. Separation of Employment

**1.1 Separation Date and Characterization.** Employee's employment with the Company will terminate effective April 16, 2026 (the "Separation Date"). The Parties agree that this separation is an involuntary termination of employment by the Company.

**1.2 Employment Relationship.** Employee acknowledges that, as of the Separation Date, Employee's employment relationship with the Company ends, and except as expressly set forth in this Agreement, Employee will no longer be entitled to any compensation, benefits, or other remuneration from the Company after the Separation Date.

---

## 2. Consideration

**2.1 Severance Payment.** In consideration of Employee's promises, covenants, and releases set forth in this Agreement, the Company shall pay Employee a severance payment in the gross amount of Sixteen Thousand Two Hundred Fifty Dollars ($16,250.00), less applicable taxes and other required withholdings (the "Severance Payment").

**2.2 Payment Timing.** The Severance Payment shall be paid in a lump sum within ten (10) business days after this Agreement becomes effective, provided that Employee has timely signed and returned this Agreement and has not revoked it pursuant to Section 11.3.

**2.3 No Other Separation Benefits.** Employee acknowledges and agrees that the Severance Payment constitutes consideration to which Employee is not otherwise entitled and that, other than any final wages, properly reimbursable business expenses, vested benefits, and any rights that cannot lawfully be waived, Employee is not entitled to any additional severance, bonus, incentive compensation, partnership payment, equity payment, or other separation-related compensation.

---

## 3. Final Pay, Benefits, and Expenses

**3.1 Final Wages.** The Company shall pay Employee all earned wages or salary through the Separation Date, less applicable deductions and withholdings, in accordance with applicable law and regular payroll practices.

**3.2 Business Expenses.** The Company shall reimburse Employee for properly documented and reimbursable business expenses incurred through the Separation Date, provided such expenses are submitted in accordance with Company policy and within any applicable deadline.

**3.3 Benefits and Continuation Coverage.** Employee's active participation in Company-sponsored benefit plans shall end in accordance with the terms of those plans as of the Separation Date or such other date required by the applicable plan terms. Employee may have the right to elect continuation of certain health coverage, if any, at Employee's sole cost and expense, under applicable federal or Maryland continuation-coverage law. The Company is not providing any employer-paid subsidy for continuation coverage after the Separation Date. Any continuation-coverage election materials shall be provided through the applicable plan administrator, carrier, broker, or other benefits administrator in the ordinary course, independently of and not conditioned on Employee's execution of this Agreement.

---

## 4. General Release of Claims

**4.1 Release.** In exchange for the consideration provided under this Agreement, Employee, for herself and for her heirs, executors, administrators, representatives, successors, and assigns, knowingly and voluntarily releases and forever discharges the Company and each of its past and present parents, subsidiaries, affiliates, predecessors, successors, assigns, members, managers, officers, directors, employees, agents, attorneys, insurers, benefit plans, and representatives (collectively, the "Released Parties") from any and all claims, demands, causes of action, liabilities, damages, obligations, and rights of every kind, whether known or unknown, suspected or unsuspected, fixed or contingent, that Employee ever had, now has, or may claim to have against any of the Released Parties arising out of or relating to any act, omission, event, circumstance, or transaction occurring on or before the date Employee signs this Agreement.

**4.2 Included Claims.** The claims released by Employee include, without limitation, claims arising out of or relating to Employee's recruitment, hiring, employment, compensation, position, duties, performance, benefits, leave, discipline, separation from employment, and any alleged ownership, compensation, or contractual rights, as well as claims arising under federal, state, and local statutes, regulations, ordinances, common law, or equity, including claims for breach of contract, wrongful discharge, retaliation, discrimination, harassment, tort damages, emotional distress, defamation, attorneys' fees, and costs, including claims under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, the Americans with Disabilities Act, the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, the Family and Medical Leave Act, Section 1981, the Maryland Fair Employment Practices Act (Md. Code, State Gov't Art. § 20-601 et seq.), and similar laws, to the fullest extent such claims may lawfully be released.

**4.3 Excluded Claims.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, this release does not waive or release: (a) claims that cannot lawfully be waived; (b) claims arising after Employee signs this Agreement; (c) claims to enforce this Agreement; (d) rights to vested benefits, if any; (e) rights to indemnification, defense, or insurance coverage under applicable law or governing documents, if any; (f) rights to participate in, cooperate with, or communicate with any governmental agency, subject to Section 5 below; (g) any claim for workers' compensation benefits; (h) any unemployment claim or right to seek continuation coverage under applicable law; or (i) any claim for unpaid wages, earned commissions, accrued but unpaid paid time off or vacation to the extent payable under applicable law or Company policy, claims under the Fair Labor Standards Act, claims under the Maryland Wage Payment and Collection Law, or any other compensation-related claim that cannot lawfully be waived or released by private agreement.

---

## 5. Covenant Not to Sue; Government Agency Carve-Out

**5.1 Covenant Not to Sue.** Except as otherwise prohibited by law, Employee covenants and agrees not to file, initiate, or voluntarily pursue any lawsuit, administrative complaint, or other legal or quasi-legal proceeding against any Released Party based on any claim released by this Agreement.

**5.2 Government Agency Communications.** Nothing in this Agreement prohibits Employee from filing a charge, complaint, or report with, or participating in an investigation or proceeding conducted by, any federal, state, or local governmental agency. However, to the fullest extent permitted by law, Employee waives any right to recover individual monetary relief or other personal recovery from any Released Party based on claims released under this Agreement. Nothing in this Agreement limits Employee's right to receive a whistleblower award, bounty, or similar recovery from the SEC, IRS, or any other governmental agency program for information provided to such agency.

**5.3 Protected Activity Savings Clause.** Nothing in this Agreement, including Sections 5, 6, 7, or 8, prohibits or is intended to prohibit Employee from communicating with, filing a charge or complaint with, participating in any investigation or proceeding before, or providing truthful information to any federal, state, or local government agency, including the EEOC, NLRB, SEC, DOL, or any similar agency; responding truthfully to legal process; discussing wages, hours, or working conditions as permitted by law; or engaging in any other conduct protected by applicable law, including Section 7 of the National Labor Relations Act.

---

## 6. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

**6.1 Confidential Information.** Employee acknowledges that, during the course of employment, Employee had access to confidential, proprietary, and non-public information belonging to the Company, including information regarding clients, prospective clients, pricing, financial information, business strategy, operations, personnel, and internal processes (collectively, "Confidential Information").

**6.2 Non-Disclosure.** Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, use, disclose, publish, or communicate any Confidential Information except as required by law or as expressly permitted by this Agreement.

**6.3 Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) becomes publicly available through no fault of Employee; (b) was lawfully known to Employee before disclosure by the Company; (c) is lawfully obtained by Employee from a third party without breach of any obligation; or (d) must be disclosed pursuant to legal process, subpoena, court order, or governmental request, provided that, to the extent legally permitted, Employee gives the Company prompt notice and reasonable cooperation.

**6.4 Permitted Disclosures.** Nothing in this Agreement prohibits Employee from discussing this Agreement with Employee's spouse, attorney, or tax advisor, provided that such person is informed of the confidential nature of the information, or from making disclosures protected by applicable law.

**6.5 Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice.** Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, Employee is hereby notified that, under 18 U.S.C. Section 1833(b), Employee will not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that: (a) is made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (b) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal. In addition, if Employee files a lawsuit for retaliation by the Company for reporting a suspected violation of law, Employee may disclose the Company's trade secrets to Employee's attorney and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, provided that Employee files any document containing the trade secret under seal and does not disclose the trade secret except pursuant to court order.

---

## 7. Non-Solicitation

**7.1 Employee Non-Solicitation.** For a period of twenty-four (24) months following the Separation Date, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, for herself or for any other person or entity, actively solicit or recruit for employment or engagement any employee of the Company with whom Employee worked directly, supervised, or had regular professional interaction during the final twenty-four (24) months of Employee's employment. This Section does not prohibit the hiring or engagement of any person who independently seeks employment or engagement without direct or indirect solicitation by Employee.

**7.2 Client Non-Solicitation.** For a period of twenty-four (24) months following the Separation Date, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or attempt to divert away from the Company any client or customer of the Company with whom Employee had material business contact or responsibility during the final twenty-four (24) months of Employee's employment, for the purpose of providing products or services competitive with those offered by the Company. This Section does not prohibit Employee from accepting business from a person or entity that independently seeks out Employee without direct or indirect solicitation by Employee.

**7.3 Reasonableness.** Employee acknowledges and agrees that the restrictions in this Section are reasonable in duration, scope, and subject matter and are necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests.

---

## 8. Non-Disparagement

**8.1 By Employee.** Employee shall not make any knowingly false, misleading, defamatory, or malicious statement about the Company or any Released Party.

**8.2 By Company.** The Company agrees that its executive leadership shall not knowingly make any false, misleading, defamatory, or malicious statement about Employee.

**8.3 Protected Conduct.** Nothing in this Section prohibits truthful testimony, legally compelled disclosures, communications with an attorney, communications with or participation before any governmental agency, or communications protected by applicable law, including protected concerted activity.

---

## 9. Return of Property; Cooperation

**9.1 Return of Company Property.** No later than the Separation Date, or such other date designated by the Company, Employee shall return all Company property, materials, records, files, equipment, devices, access credentials, and documents in Employee's possession, custody, or control, whether in physical or electronic form, including client engagement files, work papers, financial models, pricing materials, internal templates, and any Confidential Information stored on personal devices, personal email, cloud storage, or removable media, and shall not retain copies except as required by law.

**9.2 Certification and Transition Cooperation.** Within five (5) business days after the Separation Date, Employee shall certify in writing that Employee has returned Company property; deleted Company Confidential Information from Employee's personal devices, personal email, cloud storage, and removable media; and not knowingly retained copies of Company Confidential Information except as required by law. Employee also agrees to provide reasonable transition cooperation relating to Company clients, projects, relationships, and internal matters known to Employee for up to ten (10) hours during the sixty (60) days following the Separation Date. Any requested post-separation cooperation beyond de minimis transition questions shall be compensated at a mutually agreed reasonable hourly rate.

---

## 10. Neutral Reference

If the Company receives a reference inquiry regarding Employee, the Company shall provide only neutral reference information consisting of dates of employment and position held, unless otherwise required by law or authorized by Employee in writing.

---

## 11. Review Period; Revocation; Older Worker Requirements

**11.1 Review Period.** Employee shall have twenty-one (21) days from receipt of this Agreement to consider whether to sign it, although Employee may sign it sooner voluntarily.

**11.2 Advice of Counsel.** Employee is advised in writing to consult with an attorney before signing this Agreement.

**11.3 Revocation Period.** Employee may revoke this Agreement within seven (7) days after signing it by delivering written notice of revocation to the Company. This Agreement shall not become effective or enforceable until the eighth (8th) day after Employee signs it, provided that Employee has not revoked it.

**11.4 Older Worker Requirements.** Because Employee is over age 40, the Parties intend that this Agreement be interpreted and enforced in compliance with the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, as amended, and the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act. Employee acknowledges that she has been given the opportunity to consider this Agreement for the full review period stated above and to consult counsel before signing.

---

## 12. No Admission of Liability

This Agreement is entered into as a compromise and settlement of disputed or potential claims. Nothing in this Agreement constitutes or shall be construed as an admission of liability, wrongdoing, or violation of law by the Company, Employee, or any Released Party.

---

## 13. Governing Law; Venue; Remedies

**13.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Maryland, without regard to its conflict-of-law rules.

**13.2 Exclusive Venue.** Any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this Agreement, Employee's employment, or the termination of that employment shall be brought exclusively in a state or federal court located in Maryland, and each Party consents to personal jurisdiction and venue in those courts.

**13.3 Injunctive Relief and Other Remedies.** Employee acknowledges that an actual or threatened breach of Sections 6, 7, or 9 may cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages alone may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the Company may seek temporary, preliminary, and permanent injunctive relief, in addition to any other legal or equitable remedies available.

---

## 14. Miscellaneous

**14.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the complete and exclusive agreement between the Parties concerning Employee's separation from employment and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous discussions, negotiations, and understandings on that subject, except for any agreement that expressly survives and is not inconsistent with this Agreement.

**14.2 Clawback for Material Breach.** If Employee materially breaches Sections 6, 7, or 9, then, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Company shall be entitled to recover from Employee the net Severance Payment paid under Section 2.1, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

**14.3 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

**14.4 Severability; Reformation.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect to the maximum extent permitted by law. If any restrictive covenant is deemed overbroad, a court may modify it only to the extent permitted by law.

**14.5 Assignment.** Employee may not assign this Agreement or any rights under it. The Company may assign this Agreement to any successor or affiliated entity.

**14.6 Counterparts; Electronic Signatures.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and signatures transmitted electronically shall be deemed valid and binding.

**14.7 Voluntary Execution.** Employee acknowledges that Employee has carefully read this Agreement, understands its terms, enters into it knowingly and voluntarily, and is not relying on any representation not expressly set forth in this Agreement.

SEPARATION AGREEMENT AND GENERAL RELEASE

This Separation Agreement and General Release (the "Agreement") is made by and between Harbor View Consulting LLC (the "Company") and Danielle Rommè ("Employee"). The Company and Employee are referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

1. Separation of Employment

1.1 Separation Date. Employee's employment with the Company will terminate effective April 16, 2026 (the "Separation Date").

1.2 Employment Relationship. Employee acknowledges that, as of the Separation Date, Employee's employment relationship with the Company ends, and except as expressly set forth in this Agreement, Employee will no longer be entitled to any compensation, benefits, or other remuneration from the Company after the Separation Date.

2. Consideration

2.1 Severance Payment. In consideration of Employee's promises, covenants, and releases set forth in this Agreement, the Company shall pay Employee a severance payment in the gross amount of Sixteen Thousand Two Hundred Fifty Dollars ($16,250.00), less applicable taxes and other required withholdings (the "Severance Payment").

2.2 Payment Timing. The Severance Payment shall be paid in a lump sum within ten (10) business days after this Agreement becomes effective, provided that Employee has timely signed and returned this Agreement and has not revoked it.

2.3 No Other Separation Benefits. Employee acknowledges and agrees that the Severance Payment constitutes consideration to which Employee is not otherwise entitled and that, other than any final wages, properly reimbursable business expenses, vested benefits, and any rights that cannot lawfully be waived, Employee is not entitled to any additional severance, bonus, incentive compensation, partnership payment, equity payment, or other separation-related compensation.

3. Final Pay, Benefits, and Expenses

3.1 Final Wages. The Company shall pay Employee all earned wages or salary through the Separation Date, less applicable deductions and withholdings, in accordance with applicable law and regular payroll practices.

3.2 Business Expenses. The Company shall reimburse Employee for properly documented and reimbursable business expenses incurred through the Separation Date, provided such expenses are submitted in accordance with Company policy and within any applicable deadline.

3.3 Benefits. Employee's participation in Company-sponsored benefit plans shall end in accordance with the terms of those plans and applicable law. Nothing in this Agreement alters any continuation rights, if any, available under applicable law.

4. General Release of Claims

4.1 Release. In exchange for the consideration provided under this Agreement, Employee, for herself and for her heirs, executors, administrators, representatives, successors, and assigns, knowingly and voluntarily releases and forever discharges the Company and each of its past and present parents, subsidiaries, affiliates, predecessors, successors, assigns, members, managers, officers, directors, employees, agents, attorneys, insurers, benefit plans, and representatives (collectively, the "Released Parties") from any and all claims, demands, causes of action, liabilities, damages, obligations, and rights of every kind, whether known or unknown, suspected or unsuspected, fixed or contingent, that Employee ever had, now has, or may claim to have against any of the Released Parties arising out of or relating to any act, omission, event, circumstance, or transaction occurring on or before the date Employee signs this Agreement.

4.2 Included Claims. The claims released by Employee include, without limitation, claims arising out of or relating to Employee's recruitment, hiring, employment, compensation, position, duties, performance, benefits, leave, discipline, separation from employment, and any alleged ownership, compensation, or contractual rights, as well as claims arising under federal, state, and local statutes, regulations, ordinances, common law, or equity, including claims for breach of contract, wrongful discharge, retaliation, discrimination, harassment, wage payment, bonus compensation, tort damages, emotional distress, defamation, attorneys' fees, and costs.

4.3 Excluded Claims. Notwithstanding the foregoing, this release does not waive or release: (a) claims that cannot lawfully be waived; (b) claims arising after Employee signs this Agreement; (c) claims to enforce this Agreement; (d) rights to vested benefits, if any; (e) rights to indemnification, defense, or insurance coverage under applicable law or governing documents, if any; or (f) rights to participate in, cooperate with, or communicate with any governmental agency, subject to Section 5 below.

5. Covenant Not to Sue; Government Agency Carve-Out

5.1 Covenant Not to Sue. Except as otherwise prohibited by law, Employee covenants and agrees not to file, initiate, or voluntarily pursue any lawsuit, arbitration, administrative complaint, or other legal or quasi-legal proceeding against any Released Party based on any claim released by this Agreement.

5.2 Government Agency Communications. Nothing in this Agreement prohibits Employee from filing a charge, complaint, or report with, or participating in an investigation or proceeding conducted by, any federal, state, or local governmental agency. However, to the fullest extent permitted by law, Employee waives any right to recover individual monetary relief or other personal recovery from any Released Party based on claims released under this Agreement.

6. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

6.1 Confidential Information. Employee acknowledges that, during the course of employment, Employee had access to confidential, proprietary, and non-public information belonging to the Company, including information regarding clients, prospective clients, pricing, financial information, business strategy, operations, personnel, and internal processes (collectively, "Confidential Information").

6.2 Non-Disclosure. Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, use, disclose, publish, or communicate any Confidential Information except as required by law or as expressly permitted by this Agreement.

6.3 Exclusions. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) becomes publicly available through no fault of Employee; (b) was lawfully known to Employee before disclosure by the Company; (c) is lawfully obtained by Employee from a third party without breach of any obligation; or (d) must be disclosed pursuant to legal process, subpoena, court order, or governmental request, provided that, to the extent legally permitted, Employee gives the Company prompt notice and reasonable cooperation.

6.4 Permitted Disclosures. Nothing in this Agreement prohibits Employee from discussing this Agreement with Employee's spouse, attorney, or tax advisor, provided that such person is informed of the confidential nature of the information, or from making disclosures protected by applicable law.

7. Non-Solicitation

7.1 Employee Non-Solicitation. For a period of twenty-four (24) months following the Separation Date, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, for herself or for any other person or entity, solicit, recruit, induce, encourage, hire, or attempt to solicit, recruit, induce, encourage, or hire any employee or independent contractor of the Company with whom Employee had material business contact or about whom Employee obtained Confidential Information during the final twenty-four (24) months of Employee's employment.

7.2 Client Non-Solicitation. For a period of twenty-four (24) months following the Separation Date, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, divert, service, accept business from, or attempt to take away any client, customer, or actively pursued prospective client of the Company with whom Employee had material business contact, responsibility, or access to Confidential Information during the final twenty-four (24) months of Employee's employment, for the purpose of providing products or services competitive with those offered by the Company.

7.3 Reasonableness. Employee acknowledges and agrees that the restrictions in this Section are reasonable in duration, scope, and subject matter and are necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests.

8. Non-Disparagement

8.1 By Employee. Employee shall not make any knowingly false, misleading, defamatory, or malicious statement about the Company or any Released Party.

8.2 By Company. The Company agrees that its executive leadership shall not knowingly make any false, misleading, defamatory, or malicious statement about Employee.

8.3 Protected Conduct. Nothing in this Section prohibits truthful testimony, legally compelled disclosures, or communications protected by applicable law.

9. Return of Property; Cooperation

9.1 Return of Company Property. No later than the Separation Date, or such other date designated by the Company, Employee shall return all Company property, materials, records, files, equipment, devices, access credentials, and documents in Employee's possession, custody, or control, whether in physical or electronic form, and shall not retain copies except as required by law.

9.2 Transition Cooperation. Employee agrees to provide reasonable transition cooperation relating to Company clients, projects, relationships, and internal matters known to Employee. If the Company requests substantial post-separation assistance, the Parties may separately agree on reasonable compensation for such time.

10. Neutral Reference

If the Company receives a reference inquiry regarding Employee, the Company shall provide only neutral reference information consisting of dates of employment and position held, unless otherwise required by law or authorized by Employee in writing.

11. Review Period; Revocation; Older Worker Requirements

11.1 Review Period. Employee shall have twenty-one (21) days from receipt of this Agreement to consider whether to sign it, although Employee may sign it sooner voluntarily.

11.2 Advice of Counsel. Employee is advised in writing to consult with an attorney before signing this Agreement.

11.3 Revocation Period. Employee may revoke this Agreement within seven (7) days after signing it by delivering written notice of revocation to the Company. This Agreement shall not become effective or enforceable until the eighth (8th) day after Employee signs it, provided that Employee has not revoked it.

11.4 Older Worker Requirements. Because Employee is over age 40, the Parties intend that this Agreement be interpreted and enforced in compliance with the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, as amended, and the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act. Employee acknowledges that she has been given the opportunity to consider this Agreement for the full review period stated above and to consult counsel before signing.

12. No Admission of Liability

This Agreement is entered into as a compromise and settlement of disputed or potential claims. Nothing in this Agreement constitutes or shall be construed as an admission of liability, wrongdoing, or violation of law by the Company, Employee, or any Released Party.

13. Governing Law; Arbitration

13.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Maryland, without regard to its conflict-of-law rules.

13.2 Binding Arbitration. Except for claims seeking temporary, preliminary, or permanent injunctive relief relating to an alleged breach of Sections 6, 7, or 9, any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this Agreement, Employee's employment, or the termination of that employment shall be resolved exclusively by final and binding arbitration in Maryland before a single arbitrator administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Employment Arbitration Rules.

13.3 Arbitration Authority; Costs. The arbitrator shall have authority to award any remedy available under applicable law or this Agreement, except that the arbitrator may not rewrite or alter the terms of this Agreement. Fees and costs of arbitration shall be allocated as required by applicable law and otherwise in accordance with the arbitrator's award or the applicable arbitration rules.

13.4 Court Jurisdiction for Permitted Proceedings. Any court proceeding permitted under Section 13.2, including proceedings to obtain injunctive relief or to confirm or enforce an arbitration award, shall be brought in a state or federal court located in Maryland, and each Party consents to personal jurisdiction and venue in those courts.

14. Miscellaneous

14.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the complete and exclusive agreement between the Parties concerning Employee's separation from employment and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous discussions, negotiations, and understandings on that subject, except for any agreement that expressly survives and is not inconsistent with this Agreement.

14.2 Amendment. This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

14.3 Severability; Reformation. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect to the maximum extent permitted by law. If any restrictive covenant is deemed overbroad, a court or arbitrator may modify it only to the extent permitted by law.

14.4 Assignment. Employee may not assign this Agreement or any rights under it. The Company may assign this Agreement to any successor or affiliated entity.

14.5 Counterparts; Electronic Signatures. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and signatures transmitted electronically shall be deemed valid and binding.

14.6 Voluntary Execution. Employee acknowledges that Employee has carefully read this Agreement, understands its terms, enters into it knowingly and voluntarily, and is not relying on any representation not expressly set forth in this Agreement.

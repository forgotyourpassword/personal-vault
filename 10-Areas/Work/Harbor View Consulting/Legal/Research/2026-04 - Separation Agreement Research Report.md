## Useful takeaways from the opening sections
- A release needs consideration beyond amounts already legally owed.
- The release should not attempt to waive future claims or non-waivable rights.
- The agreement should preserve the employee's ability to file charges with agencies like the EEOC and NLRB even if personal monetary recovery is waived.
- COBRA / benefits continuation should be spelled out clearly, including any employer subsidy period and who administers enrollment.
- Return-of-property language should be explicit for client files, workpapers, models, and electronic copies.

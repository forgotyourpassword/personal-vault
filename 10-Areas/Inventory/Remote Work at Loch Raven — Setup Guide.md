# Remote Work at Loch Raven — Setup Guide

Tags: #project #remote-work #camping #starlink #loch-raven

## Concept
Work from the woods at Loch Raven Reservoir — short hike in, set up hammock + Starlink + solar power, work remotely for 4-6 hours on weekdays.

---

## Primary Route — Morgan Mill Road

### Parking
Loch Raven - Morgan Mill Rd Parking
99 Loch Raven Dr, Phoenix, MD 21131
Google Maps: https://maps.app.goo.gl/R67LF9MsKqmwmK4c6

### Current Destination (Michael's go-to)
Reservoir shoreline near the end of the Morgan Mill Trail
Coordinates: 39.4432877, -76.5462840
Google Maps: https://maps.app.goo.gl/LUay7mYAkFfqEcni8
Distance: ~0.75-1.0 miles one way
Elevation: Parking at ~280ft, climbs to ~340ft over Tom's Hill / Hugh's Hill, then drops to ~250ft at the water

### Trail Character
- Mix of single-track and fire road
- Moderately easy, not technical
- Rolling hills — about 100ft of climb before descending to water
- Manageable with 20+ lbs gear, wear a pack with hip belt
- Fire road sections are wide and easy; single-track near Tom's Hill is the most demanding segment

### Best Spots On This Route

OPTION A — Utility/Power Line Corridor (~0.4 miles from parking)
- A wide cleared strip running N-S through the forest
- BEST STARLINK SPOT on this route — open sky in all directions
- Hang hammock just inside the tree line on either side
- Set Starlink dish in the clearing
- Light weekday foot traffic (hunters in season, occasional dog walkers)

OPTION B — Reservoir Shoreline (the destination coordinates)
- End of the trail at the water's edge
- Open sky over the water = excellent Starlink northern exposure
- Large mature trees on the upper bank for hammock
- Reservoir views — most scenic option
- Slightly more involved setup (gear down the descent)

## Alternative Locations Worth Trying

### Merrymans Trail — MD-146 West Shore (Best Alternative Overall)

**Why it's better for Starlink:** South/west shore — dish points north over open water instead of into uphill terrain. Ideal orientation.

**Parking Option 1 — MD-146 (Main lot, recommended)**
Address: 2583 MD-146, Cockeysville, MD 21030

### Loch Raven Drive Roadside Pulloffs (Quickest Option)
- Gravel pulloffs along the paved road on the western shore

- Open shoreline, Starlink works fine

### Stone Hill Road / North Area
- Access from ~12801 Stone Hill Rd (north end of reservoir)

### Glen Ellen Trail — South Peninsula
- Access via Loch Raven Drive trailhead pulloffs
- Runs along southern reservoir arm
- More shoreline exposure = better Starlink sky
- Mature ridgeline forest for hammocking
- ~1-1.5 miles from road parking

## Starlink Setup Notes for This Location
- Deep forest = no Starlink. You MUST reach an opening.
- Utility corridor or open shoreline are the two viable zones on the Morgan Mill route
- Starlink needs ~100 degree clear view of the northern sky
- Set dish on flat ground, it self-orients
- Router + dish draw 50-75W — run from [[EnginStar 300W Power Station]] AC outlet

---

## Regulations
- Day use only (6am–sunset) — Loch Raven is a protected drinking water supply
- NO overnight camping permitted
- No fires
- Hammocking for day use is fine — use wide tree straps, no damage to bark
- Managed by City of Baltimore Bureau of Water and Wastewater + MD DNR

## Power Math

- MacBook M4 moderate load: ~15-25W
- Starlink dish + router: ~50-75W
- Combined draw: ~90W
- 296Wh battery alone: ~3-3.5 hrs (MacBook + Starlink together)
- 60W solar full sun: 40-50W usable → nearly offsets draw
- Forest / partial shade: 20-35W solar input → extends battery significantly
- MacBook only: ~10-15 hrs from full battery

## On-Site Workflow

1. Park at Morgan Mill Rd lot (99 Loch Raven Dr, Phoenix MD)
2. Hike trail south ~0.75 miles — options: stop at utility corridor OR continue to shoreline
3. Find two trees 10-15ft apart — hang hammock
4. Place solar panel in best sky exposure (separate from hammock if needed)
5. Plug solar panel into power station DC input — start charging
6. Set Starlink dish flat on ground in open area, plug into router, plug router into power station AC outlet
7. Wait 5-10 min for Starlink to orient and connect
8. Connect MacBook to Starlink WiFi, charge via USB-C PD port (60W)
9. Get in hammock, start working

## Field Reports

### April 13, 2026 — Morgan Mill / Reservoir Shoreline
**Result:** Connectivity failure — fell back to phone calls

**Starlink issue:** Dish required antenna pointing away from the water and back toward the trees/uphill slope. That direction had heavy obstructions — too many trees and terrain blocking the northern sky view. Could not get reliable connection.

**Lesson:** The shoreline spot assumes open sky OVER the water. If Starlink's required orientation points inland (uphill + trees), this spot fails. Check the Starlink app obstruction map BEFORE committing to a setup location.

**Weather:** Rain arrived around noon. Packed up and headed home early.

**Rain prep:** Well-prepared — rain jacket, rain cover for backpack, all gear in dry bag inside pack. Gear stayed dry. Rain gear worked as expected.

**Takeaway:** Next visit, try Option A (Utility Corridor) first — open sky in all directions removes the orientation problem entirely. Shoreline is scenic but dependent on Starlink pointing north over water.

**South shore theory:** Since Starlink points north, being on the NORTH shore means the dish faces back into the uphill forested terrain — exactly what happened here. A south shore location would have north pointing out over the open water, which is ideal. Merrymans Trail (MD-146 West Shore) is the best candidate to test this — flatter approach, meadow clearings overlooking the reservoir, and sitting on the south/west side.

## Known Limitations
- Video calls = high battery drain — mute camera when possible
- Starlink signal can drop under moving canopy in wind
- Maryland spring weather unpredictable — bring rain gear
- Morning briefing includes a daily "Work from the Woods" verdict on weekdays based on weather
- Shoreline spot requires Starlink to point NORTH over water — if terrain/orientation forces it inland, obstructions kill the signal. Always check the Starlink app obstruction map on arrival before committing to a full setup.
- Utility Corridor (Option A) is more reliable — open sky in all directions, no orientation dependency
- North shore (Morgan Mill) is a bad fit for shoreline Starlink — dish faces north = points back into the uphill tree-covered terrain behind you. South shore locations are better — dish faces north = points out over the open water for a clean sky view.

## Purpose

Eat in a way that supports energy, training, family life, and long-term health without turning food into another overbuilt system.

The current pattern from recent journal entries: nutrition has been loose, vegetables/protein have been low, sickness and low energy have made momentum harder, and the useful reset is small protective actions: hydrate, protein, vegetables, rest.

## North Star

Most days should include:

- Protein at each real meal.
- At least one vegetable or fruit anchor.
- Enough water that low energy is not being made worse by dehydration.
- A deliberate answer to: “Why am I eating right now?”

## Why I Eat

Track actual drivers, not moral judgments.

Observed drivers:
- Cravings for chocolate, sugar, and milk.
- Ice cream and sugary cereal can become default craving foods, especially Cocoa Pebbles and Frosted Flakes.
- Cravings usually hit hardest in the late afternoon and evenings.
- Lunch happens, but varies wildly, so late-day cravings may be partly driven by inconsistent lunch quality rather than no lunch.

Common buckets:

- Physical hunger
- Low energy
- Stress / pressure
- Convenience
- Family/social context
- Reward after a hard day
- Boredom or grazing
- Habit / autopilot

## Goals

### Baseline goals
- Get protein earlier in the day.
- Add vegetables or fruit before trying to optimize everything else.
- Reduce autopilot snacking by naming the reason before eating.
- Keep the system light enough to survive busy family/work days.

### Near-term goal
For the next 7 days: Michael will tell Hermes what he ate each day. Hermes will log the food notes, analyze the pattern, and give practical recommendations.

## Daily Food Log Workflow

When Michael reports what he ate:
- Log it in that day's journal under `## Eating Better`.
- Analyze for protein, vegetables/fruit, hydration, timing, cravings, and late-day sugar/milk/chocolate triggers.
- Recommend the smallest useful adjustment for tomorrow, not a full diet overhaul.
- Preserve the running food preferences and craving patterns in this plan note when a durable pattern appears.

## Review Cadence

Weekly review question:

> What food choices gave me better energy, and what situations pulled me into autopilot eating?

Update this note with patterns from journal entries, not aspirations.

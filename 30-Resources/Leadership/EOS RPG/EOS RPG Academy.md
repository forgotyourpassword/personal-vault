     9|# EOS RPG Academy
    10|
    11|Your job is not to *finish* EOS. Your job is to become dangerous with it.
    12|
    13|This system turns *Traction* and the Entrepreneurial Operating System into a campaign instead of a reading project. The point is practical mastery: recall, diagnosis, application, and leadership judgment.
    14|
    15|## Core Loop
    16|
    17|1. Read a section of *Traction*.
    18|2. Capture the important idea in your own words.
    19|3. Clear a quest, drill, or boss fight.
    20|4. Earn XP.
    21|5. Rank up when you can explain and apply EOS without leaning on the book like a crutch.
    22|
    23|## Win Condition
    24|
    25|You have "mastered" a concept when you can do all 4:
    26|- explain it in plain English
    27|- spot when it is missing in a real business
    28|- choose the right EOS tool for the problem
    29|- use it in Harbor View or personal operating rhythms without overcomplicating it
    30|
    31|## Character Framework
    32|
    33|Start with one primary class and one secondary class.
    34|
    35|### Primary Classes
    36|- **Visionary**: best at seeing the big picture, simplifying direction, naming what matters
    37|- **Integrator**: best at alignment, ownership, cadence, and execution
    38|- **Operator**: best at applying EOS in the real world, running meetings, tracking rocks, and clearing issues
    39|
    40|### EOS Domain Specializations
    41|See [[EOS Skill Tree]].
    42|
    43|## Stats
    44|Track these from 1-10 and update monthly.
    45|- **Clarity**: can you explain EOS simply?
    46|- **Diagnosis**: can you identify the real operating issue?
    47|- **Cadence**: can you create rhythm and follow-through?
    48|- **Accountability**: can you assign clear ownership?
    49|- **Simplicity**: can you resist adding unnecessary process?
    50|- **Execution**: can you turn insight into action?
    51|
    52|## XP System
    53|- Read a chapter and summarize it from memory: **+10 XP**
    54|- Complete a recall drill: **+5 XP**
    55|- Answer a scenario correctly: **+10 XP**
    56|- Win a boss fight: **+25 XP**
    57|- Apply a concept to Harbor View or home systems: **+20 XP**
    58|- Teach back a concept cleanly with examples: **+15 XP**
    59|- Build a real EOS artifact, like a scorecard, accountability chart, or issues list: **+30 XP**
    60|
    61|## Rank Ladder
    62|- **Level 1: Apprentice** 0-49 XP
    63|- **Level 2: Operator** 50-119 XP
    64|- **Level 3: Integrator** 120-219 XP
    65|- **Level 4: Implementer** 220-349 XP
    66|- **Level 5: Traction Warden** 350+ XP
    67|
    68|## Campaign Arcs
    69|### Arc I: Foundation
    70|- Understand why businesses get messy
    71|- Learn the 6 key components
    72|- Learn why simplicity beats complexity
    73|
    74|### Arc II: Control Panel
    75|- Use scorecards, rocks, meeting cadence, and accountability tools
    76|- Build repeatable rhythm
    77|
    78|### Arc III: Field Command
    79|- Diagnose real situations
    80|- Run IDS
    81|- Use EOS without turning into a process goblin

    93|## Suggested Play Style
    94|Best rhythm: 10-15 minutes, 3-4 times per week.
    95|
    96|A strong session looks like:
    97|- 1 recap from memory
    98|- 3 rapid-fire questions
    99|- 1 scenario diagnosis
   100|- 1 note on how this applies to Harbor View or your own systems
   101|
   102|## Anti-BS Rules
   103|- No points for highlighting lines and feeling smart.
   104|- No points for vague agreement.
   105|- No points for jargon without application.
   106|- If you cannot explain it simply, you do not own it yet.
   107|- If the tool is wrong for the problem, no XP.

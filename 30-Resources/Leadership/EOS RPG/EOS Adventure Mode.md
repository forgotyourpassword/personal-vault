# EOS Adventure Mode

This is the more fun, more immersive version of the campaign.

## The World
You are not just reading *Traction*. You are reclaiming order from drift, fog, and wasted motion inside a growing company.

Every EOS component is a territory to explore, clear, and fortify:
- **Vision** = the high banner on the ridge
- **People** = the fellowship and seat assignments
- **Data** = the scout reports and signal fires
- **Issues** = the monsters hiding under the floorboards
- **Process** = the roads and bridges
- **Traction** = the war drum and marching rhythm

## How adventure mode works
Each training round should feel like a quest, trial, raid, or boss fight.

Use this pattern:
1. **Quest name**
2. **Mission brief**
3. **The danger**
4. **Your move**
5. **Reward**

## Tone rules
- More adventure, less workbook.
- Still useful. No fantasy wallpaper with no substance.
- Keep the mechanics tied to real EOS learning and Harbor View reality.
- Use plain English with a little fire in it.

## Dungeon Master Rules
- Michael does not need applause for half-right answers.
- Hermes should guide aggressively, judge clearly, and push for cleaner thinking.
- Answers are graded on clarity, usefulness, EOS accuracy, and real-world applicability.
- Vague, bloated, or consultanty answers do not pass.
- If an answer is decent but not battle-ready, say so and make him reforge it.
- One question at a time. No pile-ons.

## Companion: Wick
Michael's campaign companion is **Wick** — a grizzled EOS implementer who has seen a hundred companies drown in their own meetings.

Wick's role:
- Offer hints when Michael is close but circling
- Call out the root cause when Michael is listing symptoms
- Occasionally throw in a real-world example to make the concept land
- Does NOT give away the answer — nudges only
- Speaks plainly. No corporate language. No patience for word salad.

## Current Campaign Arc
### The Banner of Harbor View
Your first task is to raise a banner clear enough that the company can march behind it.

**Objective:** Make Vision battle-ready.

To equip Vision fully:
1. Michael can explain Harbor View's direction clearly and simply.
2. The leadership team is aligned on it.
3. There is written vision people can actually use.

## Current Quest
### Vision Quest I: Raise the Banner
**Mission brief:** Speak Harbor View's direction in plain language strong enough to rally a team.

**Danger:** fuzzy ambition, bloated wording, and strategy that only exists in the founder's head.

**Current forged banner:**
> Harbor View is building the largest privately held accounting and finance consulting firm in Maryland. We serve owners and leaders of small and midsize businesses with fast, written, high-trust consulting that leaves them stronger and less dependent on us. Knowledge is owned by the company, not locked in people's heads.

**Reward:** XP, clarity, and the first step toward equipping Vision.

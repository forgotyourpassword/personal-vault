# EOS Tactical Question Bank - Part I

This bank is tuned for the first third of *Traction*. Use it for short training rounds.

## Tier 1: Recall
1. What is EOS trying to solve?
2. Name the 6 key components.
3. Why do companies become less effective as they grow?
4. What does EOS value more: elegant theory or useful simplicity?
5. In plain English, what problem does Vision solve?
6. In plain English, what problem does People solve?
7. In plain English, what problem does Data solve?
8. In plain English, what problem does Issues solve?
9. In plain English, what problem does Process solve?
10. In plain English, what problem does Traction solve?
11. What does "right person, right seat" actually mean?
12. Why is a scorecard better than leadership by anecdote?
13. What is a Rock?
14. What is IDS?
15. Why is documenting a few core processes better than documenting everything?

## Tier 2: Explain It Like a Human
Answer these without sounding like a management book.
1. Explain EOS to a skeptical employee in 4 sentences.
2. Explain why too many priorities kill traction.
3. Explain the difference between a job title and an accountability seat.
4. Explain why meetings often fail before the meeting even starts.
5. Explain why clarity beats charisma in leadership systems.

## Tier 3: Spot the Failure Mode
1. A team keeps saying "communication is the issue." What is the likely EOS move?
2. A leader says "everything is important this quarter." What does EOS say back?
3. A business has no consistent way to see problems early. Which component is hurting?
4. Everyone likes a person, but outcomes stay messy. Which EOS lens matters most?
5. The company has great intentions but no weekly rhythm. What component is weak?

## Tier 4: Scenario Cards
### Scenario 1
A team debates whether a problem is sales, fulfillment, or management. Nobody can isolate the actual issue.
- What EOS move brings order?

### Scenario 2
Leadership says a person "owns operations," but five major responsibilities are split informally across three people.
- What tool helps here first?

### Scenario 3
A business reviews financials monthly but still gets blindsided every week.
- What does EOS add that finance reports alone do not?

### Scenario 4
A company claims to value process, but every manager has their own version of the same workflow.
- What should be standardized first?

### Scenario 5
A weekly meeting includes updates, side stories, rabbit holes, and very little problem-solving.
- What would EOS strip out or restructure?

## Tier 5: Application Prompts
1. Which EOS component is currently the strongest in Harbor View?
2. Which component is weakest?
3. Where in your life do you most need traction right now: work, home, fitness, or personal systems?
4. What is one recurring issue that probably needs IDS instead of more talking?
5. If you built a scorecard for your own week, which 5 numbers would matter most?

## Tier 6: Lightning Round
Use these when you want fast reps.
- Rocks or wishes?
- Seat or title?
- Data or opinion?
- Root issue or symptom?
- Process or chaos?
- Traction or drift?

## Scoring
- Recall answer: **1 point**
- Good explanation: **2 points**
- Correct diagnosis: **3 points**
- Real-world application: **5 points**

## Suggested Session Formats
### 5-Minute Drill
- 3 recall questions
- 1 explanation question
- 1 lightning round

### 10-Minute Drill
- 3 recall questions
- 1 scenario
- 1 application prompt

### 15-Minute Drill
- 5 recall questions
- 2 scenarios
- 1 Harbor View application
- 1 note in [[00-Inbox/Traction]]

# EOS Harbor View Boss Fights

These are tailored for the kind of leadership, clarity, delegation, and execution friction that can show up in a growing professional-services firm.

## Rules of Engagement
Answer each with:
1. What is the actual operating issue?
2. Which EOS component is weakest?
3. Which EOS tool creates the fastest leverage?
4. What is the first move?
5. What political or human risk could derail this?

A strong answer is specific, simple, and not naive about people.

---

## Boss Fight HV-1: The Founder Gravity Well
Everyone waits for the leader to clarify priorities, approve decisions, and resolve ambiguity. Smart people move slower than they should because too much meaning and authority stays trapped at the top.

**Likely EOS lenses:** Vision, People, Traction, Accountability

**What this tests:** can you separate founder usefulness from founder bottlenecking?

**Reward:** +30 XP

---

## Boss Fight HV-2: The Good Soldier Trap
A trusted team member is loyal, hardworking, and helpful, but their seat has grown beyond their actual fit. The business keeps compensating with workarounds instead of naming the mismatch.

**Likely EOS lenses:** People, Accountability Chart, right person/right seat

**What this tests:** can you tell the truth without becoming cruel or simplistic?

**Reward:** +30 XP

---

## Boss Fight HV-3: The Meeting Tax
Leaders are in too many meetings, yet key issues linger. Meetings create motion but not enough decisions, ownership, or follow-through.

**Likely EOS lenses:** Traction, Issues, meeting cadence, IDS

**What this tests:** can you cut ceremony without losing control?

**Reward:** +30 XP

---

## Boss Fight HV-4: The Service Knowledge Maze
A service line depends on tribal knowledge, handoffs vary by person, and quality depends too much on who touched the work.

**Likely EOS lenses:** Process, People, Data

**What this tests:** can you document enough to create consistency without building bureaucracy?

**Reward:** +35 XP

---

## Boss Fight HV-5: The Priority Pileup
There are too many important initiatives at once. Each one is defensible. Together they are a traction-killer.

**Likely EOS lenses:** Vision, Traction, Rocks

**What this tests:** can you force tradeoffs and sequence work like an adult?

**Reward:** +35 XP

---

## Boss Fight HV-6: The Blindspot Board
Leaders have instincts and experience, but they are missing a short list of weekly leading indicators. Problems get discovered late, after pain is already visible.

**Likely EOS lenses:** Data, Scorecard

**What this tests:** can you choose numbers that actually matter?

**Reward:** +35 XP

# Workforce Automation

## Automation Expectations
- **36%** of companies expect at least 10% of jobs fully automated **within a year**
- **82%** expect at least 10% of jobs fully automated **within 3 years**

## Current Work Redesign
Despite expectations, **84% of companies have NOT redesigned jobs** around AI capabilities. The focus is on AI fluency rather than deep structural change.

## Talent Strategies
Most companies (53%) are simply educating employees on AI rather than rearchitecting roles:

| Strategy | Adoption |
|---|---|
| Educate workforce on AI fluency | 53% |
| Design upskilling/reskilling strategies | 48% |
| Assess target talent acquisition levels | 36% |
| Redesign career paths and mobility | 33% |
| Assess supply/demand of skill changes | 30% |
| Performance-based incentives for AI | 30% |
| Reimagine organizations on new work patterns | 30% |
| Measure worker trust and engagement | 30% |
| Change full-time/contract/gig balance | 19% |

## Worker Sentiment (Non-Technical)
| Sentiment | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Highly enthusiastic, proactively seeking | 13% |
| Open to exploring | 55% |
| Prefer not to use but will if required | 21% |
| Actively distrust and avoid | 4% |

## Key Concerns
1. **Entry-level roles at risk** — data entry, reconciliation, first-level customer support are prioritized for automation. But these are starting points for longer careers — organizations need alternate advancement pathways.

2. **Professional development pipeline disruption** — automation of entry-level work removes the traditional learning path for junior employees.

3. **Flattening organizational structures** — 53% have considered pod-based or non-hierarchical models as fewer roles require supervision of large teams. Only 16% have actually moved to such models significantly.

4. **New management roles** — supervisor and managerial roles shift from managing large teams to orchestration of human-AI teams.

## Reimagining Roles
Example from a major logistics company: "For example, in the future we would like to see AI enable today's pricing analysts to become pricing strategists." The vision is making existing workers more capable through force multipliers, not simply replacing them.

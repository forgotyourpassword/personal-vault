# Sovereign AI

## Definition
When a country—and the companies operating within it—design, train, and deploy AI under their own laws, on infrastructure they control, using locally governed data. The goal is reducing dependence on foreign vendors for critical AI capabilities. It's about **strategic independence**, not just technology ownership.

## Strategic Importance
- **83%** view sovereign AI as at least moderately important to strategic planning
- **43%** rate it as very important or extremely important
- **77%** factor AI solution's country of origin into vendor selection decisions
- **58%** build AI stacks primarily with local vendors
- **66%** express at least moderate concern about reliance on foreign-owned AI
- **22%** very or extremely concerned

## Regional Differences
| Region | Rely on foreign-sourced AI for majority of stack |
|---|---|
| Americas | 11% |
| EMEA | 32% |

The US has unique dynamics — state-level deployment considerations increasingly shape the landscape.

## Practical Implications
- Companies working across borders must build customized solutions for different markets
- Complex requirements vary by country
- Key questions: which data/workloads must remain within boundaries, where is local model hosting mandatory, how do transparency/auditability/documentation standards differ across markets
- Need policies for data residency, model retraining, cross-border flows
- Infrastructure must meet multiple regulatory regimes simultaneously

## Quote
> "I've been working with a lot of international companies that are adamant we use an in-country infrastructure... because we build it in their country. With state-run companies in particular, there is skepticism when you're using something from outside the country."

## Strategic Advantage
Companies that proactively engage with sovereign AI constraints can:
- Reduce regulatory uncertainty
- Enhance customer trust
- Position as preferred partners in sovereignty-sensitive industries
- Build strategic independence through locally controlled infrastructure (data, models, talent, ecosystem)

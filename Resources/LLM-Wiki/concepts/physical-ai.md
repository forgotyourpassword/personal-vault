# Physical AI

## Definition
AI systems that perceive the real world, make decisions, and drive physical actions through machines or control systems. Sits at the intersection of AI/ML, sensors, controls, and robotics.

## Adoption
| Region | Today (at least limited) | In 2 Years |
|---|---|---|
| Global | 58% | 80% |
| Asia Pacific | 71% | 90% |
| Americas | 56% | 77% |
| EMEA | 56% | 81% |

Asia Pacific leads adoption globally — both in breadth and depth (20% moderate+ use vs 17% Americas, 18% EMEA).

## Types with Greatest Expected Impact
1. Intelligent security systems / smart monitoring — 21%
2. Collaborative robotics (cobots) — 20%
3. Digital twins — 19%
4. IoT-driven retail — 16%
5. Autonomous logistics — 13%
6. Smart materials — 7%

## Leading Use Cases
- Package sorting and routing in warehouses
- Autonomous storage decisions for floor space optimization
- Collaborative robots on assembly lines
- Inspection drones with automated response
- Robotic picking arms
- Autonomous forklifts
- Computer vision for food/beverage inventory tracking in restaurants

## Controlled vs Open Environments
Physical AI in controlled domains (factories, warehouses) progresses much faster than open, real-world environments where challenges and risks are more complex and unpredictable.

## Total Cost of Ownership
Decision-makers must account for the full picture, not just equipment costs:
- Facility retrofits
- Sensors and robots
- Integration with existing systems/workflows
- Maintenance and spare parts
- Downtime during implementation/troubleshooting

A warehouse automation project might require hundreds of thousands in AI dev but millions in physical infrastructure, robotic systems, and facility modifications.

## Barriers vs Agentic AI
Physical AI adoption grows slower than software-based agentic AI (58%→80% vs 23%→74% in 2 years) because of:
- Higher costs and capital requirements
- Longer development cycles
- Stricter safety regulations
- Need for specialized hardware and maintenance
- Regulatory approval, industry-specific standards, liability frameworks

## Security and Trust
Unlike software AI in digital environments, physical AI interacts with people, equipment, and infrastructure in ways that pose safety risks if compromised. Raises stakes for testing, certification, and ongoing monitoring. Companies must focus on security, interoperability, and resilience.

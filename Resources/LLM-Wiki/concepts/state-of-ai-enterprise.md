# State of AI in the Enterprise 2026 (Deloitte)

Deloitte's annual snapshot of AI adoption across enterprise. Surveyed 3,235 director-to-C-suite leaders across 6 industries and 24 countries in Aug–Sep 2025. Published January 2026.

## Key Takeaways

### The Access-Activation Gap
Worker access to sanctioned AI tools grew 50% in one year (<40% to ~60%), but only 40% of users actually deploy them daily. Only 25% of firms have moved 40%+ of AI experiments to production, though 54% expect to within 3–6 months. The gap between availability and adoption is now the primary barrier to value. See [[ai-pilot-production-gap]].

### Transformation Depth
- **34%** deeply transforming business models — the only group truly reimaginationg
- **30%** redesigning processes around AI but keeping models intact
- **37%** surface-level use, little/no change to existing processes

### The Big Picture
- 25% report AI having a transformative effect (up from 12% YoY — more than double)
- 84% increasing AI investment
- 78% of leaders report greater confidence in AI
- Revenue growth remains aspirational: 74% hope to achieve it, only 20% are

### Three Trends Reshaping AI
1. **Agentic AI** — 74% plan to deploy within 2 years, but only 21% have mature governance ([[agentic-ai-adoption]])
2. **Sovereign AI** — 83% see strategic independence as important, 77% factoring country of origin into vendor decisions ([[sovereign-ai]])
3. **Physical AI** — 58% already using, 80% expect to within 2 years ([[physical-ai]])

### Governance and Risk
Data privacy/security tops concerns at 73%, followed by legal/IP compliance (50%) and governance capabilities (46%). Strategy (42%) and governance (30%) preparedness rising, but infrastructure (43%), data management (40%), and talent (20%) lagging. See [[ai-governance]].

### Workforce Impact
84% haven't redesigned jobs around AI despite 36% expecting 10%+ full automation within a year. Worker skills are the biggest barrier, yet <50% of firms significantly adjusting talent strategies. See [[workforce-automation]].
